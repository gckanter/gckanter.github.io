'Heat' Review

Sep 05, 2024 7:02 AM

![Heat Poster](https://m.media-amazon.com/images/M/MV5BYjZjNTJlZGUtZTE1Ny00ZDc4LTgwYjUtMzk0NDgwYzZjYTk1XkEyXkFqcGdeQXVyNjU0OTQ0OTY@._V1_SX300.jpg)

film-review

I think I am missing something from this movie. Expectations shape everything and somehow I got it in my head that this was a taut 90-minute action head rush. That's probably thanks to the fact that it's the film that incepted the excellent Rewatchables podcast. But instead, it’s a 2.5 hour, deeply romantic, deeply sensual film about attachment and obsession. That makes for a fascinating film, but when it’s not when you’re expecting it can deflate the balloon a little.

There also appear to be numerous apocryphal and legitimate stories surrounding the film that fuel the repeat enjoyment. One that comes to mind is that Pacino is apparently widely known as saying that he interpreted his character as being on cocaine. Learning this story one week after viewing helped his exuberant performance click into place. This is not a new phenomenon by any means, but lacking that context may have also factored into my feeling of being just plain whelmed by this film. The above anecdote from Pacino does prompt a question about what can and cannot be inferred by an audience and whether or not only strictly textual film elements should be considered in criticism.

Lastly, it’s always hard to re-calibrate when you’ve seen the derivatives of a work before the primary work. Nolan’s opening of The Dark Knight immediately comes to mind. I rewatched that scene as soon as I finished the movie, delighted to find __ serving as a link between the two movies. Nolan’s sleek opener gets to be so aerodynamic because Heat is propping it up for so many viewers. And once you’ve seen it, it’s hard to go back to the original with fresh eyes.

In short, this movie feels like a prime candidate for a review after my first re-watch where I eat every single one of these words. Looking forward to it.
